Problem

Needed a custom IMU PCB removing SPI support entirely, but the base schematic had ambiguous reference designators inherited from reused parts. The design also called for a larger XH connector to match wiring needs without growing the board's footprint.

Design and approach

Removed SPI entirely and replaced the original connectors with XH. Rebuilt the schematic and PCB layout from scratch to resolve duplicate and unclear reference designators before finalizing routing. Placed decoupling capacitors close to noise-sensitive components, researching capacitor behavior to justify each placement rather than defaulting to convention.

Challenges

The base schematic this board was adapted from had duplicate and ambiguous reference designators left over from reused parts, not obvious until mid-layout, when it became clear which components mapped to which footprints. Rather than patch around the confusion, the schematic and PCB layout were rebuilt from scratch with clean designators before finalizing routing, which cost some time upfront but avoided compounding the ambiguity into the final board.

Results

Fully manufactured and assembled with all of Triton Robotics' robots before the RoboMasters Robotics Competition. Deepened understanding of decoupling capacitor placement and behavior, as well as design practices around ground plane and via placement to preserve signal integrity.
